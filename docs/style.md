# Style and Tone Guide

## Voice

Neutral, precise, confident, unsalesy. Closer to quality education reporting or financial journalism than marketing copy. Think Reuters or The Economist tone applied to schools.

## Rules

- Short sentences. Average sentence length under 20 words.
- Active voice preferred.
- No superlatives unless backed by data ("the most expensive" is fine if it literally is).
- No puffery or marketing language.
- State facts. Let the reader draw conclusions.

## Banned words and phrases

- World-class
- Prestigious
- Renowned
- Outstanding (unless quoting an inspection report)
- Cutting-edge
- State-of-the-art
- Holistic
- Nurturing environment
- Best-in-class
- Unparalleled
- Exceptional
- Dynamic learning
- Any phrase that sounds like it came from a school brochure

## Allowed tone

- Direct and factual: "Tanglin Trust charges SGD 38,000 per year for Year 3."
- Observational: "The school has a strong academic reputation among Singapore's expat community."
- Cautious where evidence is thin: "Limited independent information is available."

## Editorial assessment style

- Only written when 3+ independent sources exist.
- Summarise what multiple sources say. Do not add new claims.
- Be specific: "Parents on Reddit and forums frequently cite strong A-level results" not "The school is well-regarded."
- Acknowledge negatives where they exist, factually: "Some parents report concerns about communication" not "The school has a bad reputation."

## Numbers and formatting

- Use USD as the primary currency with local currency in parentheses where useful.
- Use commas in numbers: 18,000 not 18000.
- Spell out numbers under 10 in body text. Use numerals for fees, rankings, and data.
- Use "Year 3" not "Grade 3" or "3rd grade" (British system).
